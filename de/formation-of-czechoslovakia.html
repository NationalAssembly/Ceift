October 28, 1918 – Formation of Czechoslovakia

On October 28, 1918, the independent Czechoslovak state was proclaimed in Prague as the successor state to the Austro-Hungarian Monarchy following the collapse of the Habsburg Empire at the end of the First World War. Wikipedia

The Prague Takeover and Assumption of Power

Before the end of the war, news spread in Prague of Austria-Hungary's conditional armistice, which triggered popular gatherings and a spontaneous seizure of power in the streets. Dejepis.com

Public affairs were taken over by the so-called Czechoslovak National Committee, led by prominent politicians such as František Soukup, Vavro Šrobár, Antonín Švehla and Alois Rašín, who ensured a peaceful transition from the Austro-Hungarian regime to the new republic. Radio Prague International

Declaration of Independence

During the day of October 28, 1918, representatives of the National Committee published a proclamation on the establishment of the independent Czechoslovak state and declared the Act on the Establishment of the Independent State, which legally enshrined the break from the Austro-Hungarian monarchy. Wikipedia

Czechoslovakia was founded as a democratic republic based on the principle of national self-determination, influenced by Wilson's Fourteen Points, and thus became one of the new states that emerged after the First World War.

Social and Labour Reforms of the New Republic

One of the first significant steps of the new state in the field of labour rights was the introduction of the eight-hour working day, which the National Committee approved in November 1918 as a preliminary measure and was subsequently enacted by the government in December of the same year. ČT24

The eight-hour working day was enacted in the Czechoslovak Republic by Act No. 91 on December 19, 1918, setting a maximum of 8 hours of work per day or 48 hours per week (a six-day working week), with rules for overtime, night work and the protection of young workers. ČT24

The Act stipulated that in factories, enterprises and institutions operated commercially or by the state, public or private associations, funds, societies, etc., employment must not last more than 8 hours in a twenty-four-hour day or at most 48 hours per week – a significant improvement in working conditions compared to the pre-war period.

The Significance of October 28 Today

October 28 is a public holiday in the Czech Republic under the name "Day of the Establishment of the Independent Czechoslovak State" and is one of the so-called order days, on which state decorations may be awarded to Czech citizens. Wikipedia

This day commemorates not only the birth of an independent national state, but also the democratic and social principles that were formally enshrined in the Czechoslovak Republic in 1918, including the first laws on the protection of labour rights and social security. Reflex
