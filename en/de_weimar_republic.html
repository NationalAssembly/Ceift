Weimar Republic (1920–1932)

The period of stabilization of the Weimar Republic was marked by French occupation of the Rhineland, hyperinflation, attempted putsch, and gradual economic recovery. Persistent crises, including the unpopular Treaty of Versailles and economic problems, gradually undermined confidence in the democratic system.
