Hitler's Rise (1932–1933)

The economic crisis of the early 1930s strengthened extremist parties. Adolf Hitler was appointed Reich Chancellor in January 1933. The Reichstag fire days later served as a pretext for abolishing fundamental civil liberties and passing the Enabling Act, which laid the foundation for totalitarian dictatorship.
