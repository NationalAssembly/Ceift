14. NOVEMBER 1918 – Revolutionary National Assembly

On the same day the provisional constitution came into effect, the Revolutionary National Assembly convened in Prague and at its very first session elected Tomáš Garrigue Masaryk as the first president of the Czechoslovak Republic. Masaryk, however, was not physically present—he was still abroad and did not arrive in Prague until December 21. The election took place on the basis of his extraordinary authority as the state's founder and leader of the foreign resistance movement. It was a gesture of clear symbolism: the republic from its first day identified with the individual who had built it through his scholarly work, political thinking, and international diplomacy.

From National Committee to Parliament

The National Committee, which declared independence on October 28 and took over state administration, fulfilled its task only provisionally. Although functional, its composition reflected pre-war conditions and lacked a democratic mandate. The Revolutionary National Assembly, while also appointed rather than elected, had a broader and more politically representative composition. Among the 256 deputies were representatives of Czech, Moravian, and Slovak political parties—from agrarians through social democrats to national democrats. However, there were no representatives of the German, Hungarian, and Polish minorities who rejected the new republic.

Masaryk as a Symbol of Freedom

Masaryk's name was in the republic's first days a synonym for freedom in the broadest sense. It was he who during the war traveled to London, Paris, Washington, and Petrograd to convince the allies to recognize the right of Czechs and Slovaks to their own state. His philosophical work, his emphasis on democracy, humanism, and truth as a political program earned him respect that transcended party boundaries. In a country where recently imprisonment had threatened for anti-Austrian statements, the president was now a man who had fought his whole life for freedom of thought and conscience.
