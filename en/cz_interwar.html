Economic Crisis and Threat (1933–1937)

The economic crisis of 1933 struck Czechoslovakia and created conditions for the growth of extremist movements. The republic attempted to defend itself by banning Nazi parties, strengthened international relationships particularly with the Soviet Union, and reinforced its defenses. Meanwhile, the country faced the death of its founding president Masaryk and the succession of Edvard Beneš.
